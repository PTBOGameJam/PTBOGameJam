SCHEDULE

Sometimes it is a good idea to layout a rough plan of development; so thats exactly what we did for the PTBO Game Jam 04. While it's not perfect and exact, much like the development process, it's dynamic but has the broad strokes.

  • 23 Feb
    Day 1
  • 24 Feb
    Day 2
  • 25 Feb
    Day 3
5:30 PM
Early Registration

Drop by and sign in and register, deal with paperwork, and get your badge (and SWAG!), making your life (and ours) easier Saturday morning. We will have someone at the registration table until ~9:00 PM to answer questions and get you settled.

6:00 PM - 8:00 PM
Youth Educational Block: Scratch

You have got to start somewhere; and there is no better place to start making games then with Briar Jamieson teaching a great foundation in Scratch. Keeping in line with the Adult Education Block, participants will learn the foundations of making a Frogger clone with Scratch.

6:00 PM - 8:00 PM
Adult Education Block: How to make a Frogger Clone?

Arin Blue, Matthew Davey and Robert French guide a 2 hour mini-instructional (code-along-style) session about the steps and overall process involved in making a Frogger clone in Unity.

8:00 PM - 9:00 PM
Adult Education Block: BREATHE Postmortem

Matthew Davey talks about what went right and what went horribly wrong during the production of BREATHE in the Unreal Engine.

8:00 AM
Doors Open

The doors open on PTBO Game Jam 04, the start of another fun filled adventure. Come early and get your fort setup because we are going to be here for a while!

8:30 AM
PTBO Game Jam 04 Intro

Matthew Davey takes to the mic to welcome everyone to the fourth installment of the PTBO Game Jam. Inviting present sponsors to speak, before the official event kick off. There might be a PowerPoint presentation involved, there might not. One never knows what to expect during this block of time.

9:00 AM
The Clock Starts

The 32-hour timer begins, the livestream gets going on Twitch and we are off to the races. Let the pressure begin and the temperature rise as the laptop powering the stream starts running at 99% usage for the next 32 hours. Will it hold? Nobody knows!

11:00 PM
Putt For SWAG!

The awesome folks at Unity have been providing jammers with awesome SWAG across all of our events. This time they sent some especially nice things to give out. Naturally, we gamified that process. Jammer's will be able to face a mini putt challenge with a twist to win prizes (while supplies last!).

11:30 PM
Pizza-For-Jammers

Who remembers that fun pizza order we did late into the night at 03? The community spoke, and we worked tirelessly to make it happen. We proudly announce the return of Pizza-For-Jammers. A special thank you to all of our sponsors AND the awesome team at Aramark for making this happen. We're going with the standard fair of topping selections. We will not guarantee any dietary concerns revolving around them; we will try to get a few vegetarian pizzas.

3:00 AM
Mystery Challenge

Who doesn't like a mystery challenge in the middle of the night when sleep deprevation is starting to kick in? Participation is optional, but bragging rights will last a life time (or till the next event)!

5:00 PM
Done! Let the Rest & Relaxation Begin!

At this point we all will most likely need a bit of a mental break, and depending if you were here the entire time a shower.

5:00 PM - 6:00 PMOpen To The Public
Show & Tell

Time to see what everyone else was doing while you were hyper focused on your own game. This portion of the event is open to the public and media to come and take a look at what was made.

6:00 PM
Wrap Up & Goodbyes

You don't have to go home, but you can't stay here. Matthew Davey will give his closing remarks and the work on the next PTBO Game Jam will begin.

Dates and times are subject to change without notice. Please refer to this page for the most up-to-date information.
