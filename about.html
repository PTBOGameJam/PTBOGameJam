The Basics

The PTBO Game Jam is a bi-annual not-for-profit event managed by industry professionals hosted in beautiful Peterborough Ontario, Canada. It is a gathering of people from all walks of life; from seasoned game developers and students just entering the industry, to hobbyists and enthusiasts all eager to engage and see what they can produce in a short timeframe.

Originally created in July 2016 by Matthew Davey with the support of dotBunny. The intent was to foster and support the local video game development industry, as well as develop a community that would eventually be able to take over in organizing the event. It only took a matter of weeks before other awesome 'like-minded' individuals were volunteering their support for this noble cause.

Getting Shit Done™ is the team's ethos — demonstrating what a collection of skilled professionals can accomplish when united under a common banner.

What Is A Game Jam?

A game jam is a gathering of game developers for the purpose of planning, designing, and creating one or more games within a short span of time, usually ranging between 24 and 72 hours. Game developers are generally made up of programmers, game designers, artists, and others in game development-related fields.

Industry

We work with industry leaders to create meaningful and impactful events that introduce talented individuals to the video games industry, as well as compliment already existing professionals.

Education

We offer a block of time at the start of our events dedicated to learning opportunities, as well as a question and answer period with industry professionals.

Exposure

We provide an outlet for game creators to show off their work and get noticed by media and financial backers.
